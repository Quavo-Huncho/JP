Nnamdi Azikiwe



Nnamdi Azikiwe is a Nigerian hero and legend who helped Nigeria secured their independence in 1960.
He later served as the first presidend of the Federal Republic of Nigeria which existed from 1963 to 1966 and is now referred as the "Father of Nigerian Nationalism"

About the Hero

Nnamdi Benjamin Azikiwe is one the greatest man that ever live in Nigeria whose origin is Anambra state in Nigeria, the Eastern part of the country. He was born on 16th of November, 1904 and died on 11th of May, 1996 at the age of 91 years in Enugu state after a brief illness. Zik, as porpularly referred grew up in the Northern part of Nigeria and learned how to speak Hausa which is the main language of the Northern part of the country. He was later sent to live with his aunt and grandmother in Onitsha (the Eastern part of Nigeria and his parental homeland) where he learned how to speak Igbo which is the main language of the Eastern part of the country. He later moved to Lagos when he became adult where he learned how the Yoruba language which is the main language of the Western part of Nigeria which made him to know how to speak the main three laguages of Nigeria.

Azikiwe later travelled to the United States where was known as Ben Azikiwe and attented Storer College, Columbian University, the University of Pennsylvania and Howard University. He contacted the colonial authorities with a request to represent Nigeria at the Los Angeles Olympics. He returned to Africa in 1934, where he began work as a journalist in the Gold Coast. In British West Africa, he advocated Nigerian and African Nationalism as a journalist and a political leader. His political parties were National Council of Nigeria and the Cameroons (NCNC) and Nigerian People's Party (NPP)

Nnamdi Azikiwe first got married to Flora Ogoegbunam in 1936 who later died in 1983. He later got married to Uche Ewah in 1973. Azikiwe has seven children whose names are Chukwuma Azikiwe, Emeka A. Azikiwe, Nwachukwu Azikiwe, Ngozi Azikiwe, Molokwu Azikiwe, Uwakwe Azikiwe and Jayzik Azikiwe.
